CMP Intercontinental Hotel Church

Location: Taichung, Taiwan

Program: Religious & Hospitality

Year: 2023

Team: John Jiang

The CMP Intercontinental Hotel Church in Taichung represents a unique fusion of sacred architecture and luxury hospitality design. The chapel, nestled within the hotel complex, creates a serene sanctuary that serves both hotel guests and the local community. The design draws inspiration from traditional Christian architectural elements while incorporating contemporary interpretations and local cultural influences.

The structure features a dramatic ascending form that creates a powerful sense of verticality, drawing the eye and spirit upward. Natural light plays a crucial role in the design, with carefully positioned skylights and clerestory windows that create ever-changing patterns of light and shadow throughout the day. The material palette combines warm wood tones with polished stone and delicate metal detailing, creating an atmosphere of refined spirituality.

The interior space is designed to be both intimate and uplifting, with flexible seating arrangements that accommodate both small private ceremonies and larger gatherings. Acoustic considerations have been carefully integrated into the design to enhance both spoken word and musical performances. The project demonstrates how sacred spaces can be meaningfully integrated into contemporary commercial developments while maintaining their spiritual integrity and architectural significance.
